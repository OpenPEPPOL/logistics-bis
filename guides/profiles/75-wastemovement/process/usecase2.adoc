== Use case 2 â€“ Domestic transport of polluted waste. A Movement Document is required by Authorities to follow the waste from loading 
to unloading.


When moving polluted waste within the borders of a country, authorities must not be informed in advance. But a Waste Movement document must
follow the wast from start to end of the movement, and at all times during the movement the authorities can request to see it.
If using a digital Waste Movement Document, the involved parties' applications must be enabled to display the Waste Movement Document 
upon request.
[cols="1,5",options="header",]
|====
|*Use Case number* |2
|*Use Case Name* |Domestic transport of polluted waste.
|*Use Case Description* a|
Describes a complete process to move polluted waste from the Despatch location to a Disposal facility when movement is within 
the borders of a country.
All involved parties must add a signature to the common Waste Movement Document - Waste Producer Party (Despatching party), 
Transporting Party and the Receiving Party.
Information about Type of Waste, Quantity/Weight, Despatch address and Destination is required in the Waste Movement document.
When the Waste Movement Document is signed also by the Receiver - the Disposal facility - it normally should be returned to the 
Waste Producer Party as a confirmation of that the polluted waste has arrived to the correct place and that responsibility is taken over.

The use of a Waste Movement document is normally a requirement from authorities, but using a digital Waste Movement document also adds 
benefits for parties using it. The transmission of information about the content of the transport - type of waste, quantity/weight 
destination etc - in the Waste Movement document will improve data quality and decrease manual work since no manual entry will be 
required if receiving a digital version of it.

Another benefit of using a digital Waste Movement Document is that a receiver can not accept waste that is not destined to that 
Disposal facility. If the driver went to the wrong facility, he will not be allowed to unload the waste there since the receiver has 
not received the Waste Movement document.

|*Parties involved* a|
Sender (In UBL: SenderParty) (Is different depending on which step in the process we now are in. See flow description below.)

Receiver (In UBL: ReceiverParty) (Is also different depending on the step in process. See flow description below.)

Waste Producer (In UBL: WasteProducerParty) (Responsible for the waste and issuer of the original Waste Movement Document.)

Notifier (In UBL: NotifierParty) (Responsible for the waste or his/her representative.)

Disposal Facility (In UBL: DisposalFacilityParty) (The Receiver of the Waste.)

Carrier party (In UBL: CarrierParty) 

Document Distribution party (In UBL: Party) (Involved parties that wish to receive a copy of the final version of the Waste 
Movement document may add themselves as a party here before sending the Waste Movement document on to next party in line.)

|*Pre conditions* a|
The Waste Movement document is created and signed by the Waste Producer Party or someone he/she has authorized to do it. It is created 
as a result of loading the truck that is about to move the polluted waste.
The Waste Movement document is sent to the driver of the truck and signed by this driver before he leaves.

|*Post conditions* a|
The Waste receiver - the Disposal facility - will receive the Waste Movement Document from the driver of the truck once signed by him/her.
When the document is finished, the receiver will return it to the Waste Producer - the responsible party.

|*Assumptions* a|
. The Waste Producer and the Carrier has an agreement about moving the polluted waste.
. The Waste Producer and the Disposal Facility has an agreement of taking care of and responsibility over the Waste.
. The Sender and Receiver of the Waste Movement Document may or may not have any business relationship with eachother. The Waste Movement
Document must be possible to transfer in both situations.


|*The flow* a|
. A truck arrives to the Despatch Location to load the polluted waste.
. The Waste Producer (or a party authorized by him) loads the truck, creates a Waste Movement document, signs it and sends it to 
the Carrier Party, which immediately forwards the document to the driver.
. The driver from the Carrier Party signs the Waste Movement document before leaving the Despatch Location and sends it on to the 
Receiver - the Disposal Facility. 
. The driver must be able to display the document if authorities request to see it during transport.
. The Disposal Facility must have a received signed document before accepting the polluted waste. The quantity/weight until now is
estimated but now, before unloading, the real quantity/weight must be determined and update the Waste Movement Document.
. Sometimes the type of waste is re-classified by the Receiver, and also that information should update the Waste Movement document.
. When all amendments are made, the Waste Movement document is returned to the Waste Producer - the Responsible Party.
. All involved parties in the process can add themselves as in-copy receiver of the final version of the Waste Movement document.
The Waste Producer will send out copies to all parties having requested that.


|*Result* a|
. The Authorities demands are fulfilled.
.. All involved parties sign the same document.
.. The document is visible during the whole way of the movement.
.. The Waste Producer have full control of what has happened with the waste.

. Compared with a paper-based process there are several benefits for involved parties:
.. People can stay in their vehicles and work with digital documents and do not need to "run around" with paper documents
in a dangerous environment.
.. All involved parties will have the same, correct, information about the waste movement.
.. It will not be possible to unload the waste in the wrong facility.
.. All required information can be retrieved from the Waste Movement document and nothing need to be manually entered 
(unless you need to change something).
